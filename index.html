'Narcan Behind Every Bar': How NYC Nightlife is Leading The Forefront in Fighting Against Opioid Overdoses

October 7, 2022. By Mariana Martínez Barba

The presence of fentanyl in NYC’s drug supply continues to rise, causing a number of opioid deaths. Contrary to what the public may think, 1% of Opioid overdoses only occur in nightlife precincts. However, across the five boroughs exists communities and organizations that are working to promote safer alternatives to preventing overdoses - with nightlife at the forefront of this movement. Just recently, Brooklyn Council Member Chi Ossé along with 36 co-sponsors have passed the “Nightlife Opioid Antagonist Program.” This program ensures that naloxone or narcan, an opioid overdose reversal drug, will be available to all bars and nightclubs beginning January 23rd 2022. “Narcan is a better safe than sorry scenario, we are taking advantage of the fact that (nightlife) and these spaces are opportunities where people can look out for one another and educate each other,” commented Ariel Palitz, Senior Executive Director of the Office of Nightlife.

Key Facts:

The first three months of 2022 saw NYC emergency services first responders administer naloxone 2,240 times.

According to the CDC, more than 104,000 Americans died due to a drug overdose between September 2020 and September 2021.

A large-scale national study showed that opioid overdose deaths decreased by 14 percent in states after they enacted narcan access laws.

Naloxone (narcan) quickly reverses an overdose by blocking the effects of opioids. It can restore normal breathing within 2 to 3 minutes in a person whose breath has slowed, or even stopped, as a result of opioid overdose.
